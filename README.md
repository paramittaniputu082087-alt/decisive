Decisive

Decisive is a decision intelligence stack designed to support structured, accountable, and repeatable decision-making in complex organisations.

It provides a framework to model decisions, evaluate options, record assumptions, and trace outcomes—turning critical decisions into transparent and auditable processes.

Rather than relying on informal discussions or fragmented approvals, Decisive enables organisations to treat decisions as structured systems that can be analysed, governed, and improved over time.

Why Decisive

Many organisations make critical decisions without:

clear decision structure

documented reasoning

traceable accountability

consistent evaluation criteria

As a result, decisions become difficult to review, explain, or improve.

Decisive addresses this gap by transforming decision processes into explicit, traceable systems that support governance, transparency, and organisational learning.

Core Principles
Structured Decisions

Every decision follows a defined model that clarifies objectives, options, constraints, and evaluation criteria.

Traceability

Inputs, assumptions, and outcomes are recorded so that decisions can be audited and reviewed.

Accountability

Each decision has clear ownership, rationale, and defined review paths.

Repeatability

Successful decision patterns can be reused across teams and organisational contexts.

What Decisive Provides

Decisive enables organisations to:

Model complex decisions in a structured framework

Evaluate options using defined criteria and scoring logic

Record decision assumptions and reasoning

Maintain traceable records of outcomes and impacts

Improve governance across strategic and operational decisions

Example Applications

Decisive can support decision processes in areas such as:

Strategic planning and leadership decisions

Risk and compliance review processes

Product and technology governance

Investment and resource allocation

Policy and operational decision workflows

System Concept

Decisive treats decisions as a structured lifecycle rather than isolated events.

Example lifecycle:

Decision Context
      ↓
Option Modelling
      ↓
Evaluation & Scoring
      ↓
Decision Selection
      ↓
Outcome Tracking
      ↓
Review & Learning

This allows organisations to move from informal decision-making to systematic decision governance.

Status

design validation

discussion with collaborators

exploration with potential enterprise adopters

Roadmap (High Level)
